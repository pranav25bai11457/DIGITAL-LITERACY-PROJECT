# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Think before you post.**
   Ask yourself — would I be okay if my professor, parent, or a future employer saw this? If not, don't post it. It sounds obvious, but in the moment it's easy to forget.

2. **Keep your profile photo and bio reasonably professional.**
   You don't have to be boring, but your LinkedIn and even your public Instagram can be seen by recruiters. A decent photo goes a long way.

3. **Give credit when you share someone else's work.**
   Reposting a meme or a graphic without tagging the original creator is honestly just bad manners — and on platforms like Instagram, it can get your post taken down anyway.

4. **Use privacy settings.**
   Separate what's for friends and what's public. Most platforms let you control this per post or per account. It's worth five minutes to go through your settings.

5. **Be respectful in comment sections, even when you disagree.**
   You can have a strong opinion without being rude. How you argue online says a lot about you, and those threads stick around longer than you'd expect.

6. **Log out of shared devices.**
   At a library, a friend's laptop, a college computer lab — always log out. Sounds simple, but a lot of account hacks happen because of this exact thing.

7. **Fact-check before sharing news or statistics.**
   Misinformation spreads fast. If something sounds shocking or too perfectly timed, take 60 seconds to check a reliable source before hitting repost.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't post in the heat of the moment.**
   Rant drafted at 2am about a professor, a classmate, or an exam? Save it as a draft, sleep on it, and delete it in the morning. Future-you will be relieved.

2. **Don't share your location in real time.**
   Posting "at the library till 11pm" or tagging your exact location every time you go out is a safety risk. Share the memory after, not the live update.

3. **Don't use social media to sort out personal conflicts.**
   Vague-posting about someone, starting drama in comment sections, or calling people out publicly usually makes things worse. Sort it offline.

4. **Don't ignore red flags in DMs.**
   Strangers asking for personal details, money, or "opportunities" that seem too good to be true — trust your gut. Block and report, no guilt needed.

5. **Don't treat everything as a personal platform during class or exams.**
   Scrolling through Instagram during a lecture or sharing exam content while the paper is still ongoing is disrespectful and, in the second case, potentially a disciplinary issue.

6. **Don't post confidential information.**
   If you're interning somewhere or working on a group project, don't share internal documents, client names, or anything you were trusted with privately.

---

## A Quick Note

None of this means you have to sanitise your entire personality online. Social media is genuinely fun and a great way to stay connected. The point is just to be a bit intentional — your digital footprint follows you, and it's a lot easier to manage it now than to explain it later.
