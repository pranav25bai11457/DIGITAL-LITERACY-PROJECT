================================================================
  💳  UPI & FINANCIAL SAFETY  ★ PRIORITY TIPS
================================================================

☐  1.  Never scan a QR code to "receive" money — that's not
        how UPI works.

        QR codes and payment links are used to SEND money, not
        collect it. If a buyer asks you to scan a code so they
        can pay you — stop. You're about to pay them. Receiving
        money only needs your UPI ID or phone number, nothing
        else.

        ★ Financial/UPI Safety Tip

☐  2.  Always verify the credit is in your BANK — not just in
        a screenshot or a notification.

        Before giving away any product or service, open your
        bank app and confirm the money is actually there.
        WhatsApp payment screenshots can be faked in minutes.
        A notification on your screen is NOT the same as money
        in your account. When in doubt, wait and check your
        statement.

        ★ Financial/UPI Safety Tip

================================================================
  🔒  ACCOUNT & DEVICE SECURITY
================================================================

☐  3.  Use a different password for every important account.

        When one service gets hacked, attackers automatically
        try that password on Gmail, Instagram, and your bank
        app. Use a free password manager like Bitwarden or
        Google Password Manager — you only need to remember
        one master password.

☐  4.  Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA) on Gmail,
        Instagram, and your bank app.

        This adds a one-time code step before anyone can log
        in — even if they already have your password. Takes
        2 minutes: Settings → Security → Two-Factor Auth.

☐  5.  Check which apps have access to your camera,
        microphone, and location.

        Settings → Privacy → Permissions. A torchlight app
        with microphone access is a red flag. Revoke anything
        that doesn't make sense for what the app does.

================================================================
  🎣  PHISHING & SOCIAL ENGINEERING
================================================================

☐  6.  Before clicking any link in WhatsApp or email,
        look at the actual URL.

        Fraudsters build sites like "sbi-help.in" or
        "hdfc-customercare.net" that look real. The actual
        SBI website is sbi.co.in. On mobile, hold the link
        (don't tap) to preview the address first.

☐  7.  A caller knowing your name and bank doesn't make
        them legitimate.

        Data breaches have made personal info easy to buy.
        Real bank employees NEVER ask for your OTP, PIN, or
        full card number over a call. Hang up and ring the
        official bank number yourself.

================================================================
  🌐  ONLINE BEHAVIOUR & PRIVACY
================================================================

☐  8.  Keep your social media profiles private — especially
        if your phone number is visible anywhere.

        A public profile can reveal your college, city, and
        daily routine — enough for a convincing impersonation
        call. Remove your phone number from public view on
        all platforms.

☐  9.  Don't post photos of tickets, ID cards, or transaction
        screenshots publicly.

        Boarding pass barcodes contain your booking reference.
        Bank screenshots show your UPI ID or account number.
        Share these only with people who genuinely need them,
        over private chat.

================================================================
  📢  KNOW HOW TO REPORT
================================================================

☐  10. Save the cybercrime helpline number RIGHT NOW —
        before you ever need it.

        National Cyber Crime Portal : cybercrime.gov.in
        Helpline                    : 1930  (24×7)

        If you or someone you know is defrauded online, call
        1930 immediately. The faster you report, the better
        the chance of freezing the transaction before the
        money moves. You can also file a complaint fully
        online — no police station visit required.
